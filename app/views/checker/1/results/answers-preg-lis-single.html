Because you're pregnant or have had a baby in the last 12 months:

You get free:

  • NHS prescriptions You must have a valid exemption certificate
  • NHS dental check-ups and treatment

How to get your help

To get free prescriptions

You may need to show a valid maternity exemption certificate. Get an application form from your midwife, GP or health visitor.

You can apply while you're pregnant and up to a year after your baby was born.

Loss of a baby- prescriptions

If you lost your baby after the 24th week of your pregnancy, you can still apply for a maternity exemption certificate.

If you have a miscarriage, abortion or stillborn birth you can use your maternity exemption certificate until it expires.

To get free dental care

You may need to show proof of your pregnancy, or that you have given birth in the past 12 months. You can show:

  • a valid maternity exemption certificate
  • a MATB1 certificate issued by your midwife or GP
  • your child's birth certificate
  • a notification of birth form â€“ the midwife who delivers your baby will give you this form
  • a stillbirth certificate- issued by your local registrar of births, marriages and deaths

Loss of a baby- dental care

If you lost your baby before the 24th week of your pregnancy, you're not entitled to free dental treatment.


Apply for further help

You could be entitled for further help depending on your circumstances

Your household income will be taken into account as well as your council tax and housing costs.

You don't have to be claiming benefits to apply.

Any help you get also covers your partner and children under 19.

You may also get help paying for:

  • NHS sight tests
  • new glasses and contact lenses
  • repairing or replacing your glasses and contact lenses
  • NHS wigs and fabric supports
  • travel for NHS treatment
